Research

I build models and algorithms that capture how people choose routes and modes in congested, multi-modal networks, and I study how learning-based methods can scale those models to realistic city-sized graphs. Three threads run through my work:

  1. 01

    Multi-modal traffic assignment

    Network equilibrium with combined travel modes — car, bus, metro, and their transfers. Network-GEV representations, hyperpath graphs, and two-phase solution algorithms.

  2. 02

    Multi-agent reinforcement learning

    Markov-game formulations of dynamic traffic assignment. Mean-field deep Q-learning with hybrid sequential reward feedback to reach stable Nash equilibria across heterogeneous modes.

  3. 03

    LLM reasoning over graph topology

    Graph-as-token representations, cross-modal contrastive learning between graphs and natural language, and context compression for spatial reasoning at inference time.
